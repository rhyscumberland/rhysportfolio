The creation of my board game:

Inspiration:

I began my research by studying and playing some of the most popular and best sold games of the last 50 years, I was drawn to more 1v1 card battling games such as Pokémon, magic the gathering and top trumps. I like the idea of games without a table top, to be able to make the product more transportable and affordable as production costs will be lower due to less printing being needed to produce the game. I was specifically interested in making a more refined top trumps like game but with multiple card being drawn each hand to create a team similar to Pokémon.

Game concept:

Drawing from my inspirations I have come up with a concept of a game that is going to draw fans from the previous card games but is a fresh new concept that will entice and excite new and veteran card game players. I’ve Incorporated my passion for music into the card game by making the characters on my card are interpretations of 30 of the most iconic and popular musicians of the modern era, to make the card game appealing to my target audience of older children to young adults, 8 to 18 years old I utilised a more cartoon art style. The rules of the game are, 3 players play against each other by shuffling and splitting the deck equally then each player draw 4 cards from the deck then alternate choosing an attribute category of all for cards and adding them together and comparing the final total of all 4 card against the opponent whoever has the higher number gets to choose a card out of the other players hand and add it to the bottom of their deck, then after each round all cards are retuned to each players individual decks and are shuffled again, then each player draws 4 cards again, the game continues as previously stated until 1 player has all the cards and their opponent has none.

Card design:

The base background design of the cards is a stone wall with gold framing and red curtains and a red banner to represent a stage that the “musicians” would theoretically be performing on, also I have incorporated musical notes for obvious reasons to make the card design more musically themed, having the notes go across the card but under the musician creates a level of depth to the design giving the impression of the musician being a 3D model coming out of the frame, this is also demonstrated by the shadows behind the “musicians”. The use of “times new roman” as the font is to portray a more formal style to look cohesive with the gold and deep red design elements in the curtain and gold trim of the card. All cards have 5 attributes, ability, popularity, swagg, creativity, attitude and a final attribute of an overall of all the stats added together, each stat is what I believe the real world musicians. Also added additional information for the musicians displaying the artist current age or the age they where when they passed away, and showing the genre of music they play. Each 30 designs of the musicians are stylistically different to fit with the style of the real life counterparts, some are similar styles as I believe some musicians have the same style as others. Creating a spoofed version of each real life musicians image and and name adds a comedic aspect to the card game that will appeal to my target audience for the game, but even with an increased caricature style of each the musicians I believe that I have captured the likeness of the real life counterparts in each one of my cards without being to specific, as to avoid copyright infringement.

Goals for the game:

My personal goals for this card game to to create an audience for cards games and get my target audience the youth interest in card games as in the modern era card games have been pushed to the side line and become practically obsolete due to the growing popularity of video games, I believe it is a shame that the board game has become a lost art form and is not a common used as fun recreation for family’s and friends, with an added bonus of introducing a young audience to new music and musicians through a fun card game.

Production:

After designing all the cards digitally I have been able to double side print my design onto 2.5 inch by 3.5 inch card which is the average size for playing cards, printing 31 cards with 30 of the cards being playing cards and 1 information card informing the player on how to play said game cost an average of 2 pounds per pack of cards, I have utilised a plastic case to act as storage and packaging for the cards the case has a see through front cover so I can place the first card backwards as that will display the logo design that is on the back of all the cards to potential consumers hopefully to entice the into buying my game and playing it, having the front cover be see through also cuts down on product cost as I wouldn’t have to pay for a logo design on the plastic of the case, by cutting down the cost of the case by having it blank has lowered the cost exponentially and making the average cost of the case 2 pounds 50 making. for the plastic case cover colour i have decided on red to fit in with the theme of red and gold formal stage design that is on the cards to make a cohesive theme through the whole game. The total cost of the production for 1 set of my card game is 4 pounds and 50p and this could be reduced if decide to mass produce the game, the cost will go down by around 25% making profit margin quite profitable as I will be selling the game for around 9 pounds, which is around the average price for similar card games.

Conclusion:

I am extremely pleased with the results of my card game I feel I have done my best with all aspects of the journey making my game from the digital design elements at the start and the printing and production of the card game, I feel it stylistically fits with the theme I was trying to display, that I believe will appeal to my target audience of older children to young adults. And now I await feedback from some of the people I have selected to trial my game and then hopefully, eventually I will receive positive feedback from future customers of my card game.
